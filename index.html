01 / prologue

Heat Is a Debt the Ocean Has Been Paying

I thought the ocean was far away until the heat entered my room. In Colima, Mexico, Pacific heat moves between the coast, the city, and the air I breathe.

02 / the unseen ocean

The floor gives way.

Distance was only an illusion. Every breath, cloud, meal, and hot afternoon was already connected to water I could not see.

03 / breath layer

Before I speak, something microscopic has already given me air.

Tiny drifting life turns light into oxygen. The ocean sustains even the people who have never touched it that day.

Marine life produces a major share of the oxygen we breathe.

04 / coral city

A city I never built keeps lending me color.

Fish cross the reef like messages. The ocean is not a background; it is a living architecture of shelter, food, memory, and motion.

05 / mangrove shield

Roots hold the coast the way hands hold a door in a storm.

Protection is not only a wall. Sometimes it is a forest standing in water, slowing waves before they become disaster.

Reefs and mangroves protect coastlines, but they weaken under extreme heat.

06 / heat ledger

Every absorbed degree is written somewhere.

The ocean has buffered our emissions and heat, but a buffer is not an eraser. The debt keeps moving through current, color, and life.

The ocean absorbs most of the excess heat trapped by climate change.

07 / bleaching and silence

Protection is not permission.

When the reef turns pale, the story stops sounding like abundance. What protected us begins asking to be protected back.

08 / no-water future

When the water leaves, heat is no longer weather. It is architecture.

A dry city is not separate from a wounded ocean. The crisis travels inland until the room, the street, and the sky all remember it.

09 / reciprocity

The ocean has been paying a debt it did not create. The page will not breathe again until the gesture changes direction.
